1. The Friction
SECTION HEADING
The Friction
BODY COPY
India had ~1.4 billion people but fewer than 80 million active credit-card holders.
Students, fresh graduates, gig workers, and young professionals had no formal credit history — traditional banks wouldn't issue them cards.
Result: 300+ million new-to-credit people locked out of building a credit score, pushed toward informal or high-cost credit.
2. The Founder(s)
SECTION HEADING
The Founder(s)
BODY COPY
Founded January 2016 by Rajan Bajaj (with Deepak Malhotra) as SlicePay / Garagepreneurs Internet Pvt Ltd.
Bajaj: civil engineering graduate, IIT Kharagpur; started as a business analyst intern at Walmart.
Earlier founded Mesh Internet, which failed — that experience shaped his approach to slice.
Personal connection: wanted to give India's youth easier, transparent credit access and a way to start building a credit score early.
3. The Business Model
SECTION HEADING
The Business Model
BODY COPY
2016–2022: prepaid card + credit line. Revenue from merchant subvention (Amazon, Flipkart no-cost EMIs), card interchange, and EMI interest.
June 2022: RBI banned non-banks from loading credit lines onto prepaid instruments — broke the core model overnight.
Pivot: obtained its own PPI licence; shifted to term and unsecured loans via NBFC subsidiary Quadrillion Finance.
Since 2024, as a small finance bank: net interest income from deposits and loans, fee income, plus lending and payments revenue.
Who pays: cardholders/borrowers (interest, fees), merchants (subvention, interchange), depositors indirectly fund the loan book.
4. Geography & Scale
SECTION HEADING
Geography & Scale
BODY COPY
Started in Bengaluru, 2016, targeting metro students and young professionals.
300,000+ customers across 30+ Indian cities by 2021; crossed $1 billion valuation (unicorn) on 29 November 2021.
Peak: 400,000+ cards issued in a single month — more than any other Indian fintech or bank.
Peak valuation ~$1.5–1.8 billion (2022–23), before the RBI rule change hit the business.
September 2022: acquired a 5% stake in North East Small Finance Bank (Guwahati, Assam) for $3.4 million.
Today: nationwide as slice Small Finance Bank — 20+ million registered users, 3,000+ employees, HQ Bengaluru, registered office Guwahati.
5. Status Today
SECTION HEADING
Status Today
BODY COPY
Still operating and growing. Merged with North East Small Finance Bank (completed October 2024) to become slice Small Finance Bank.
Rajan Bajaj approved by the RBI as MD & CEO of the merged bank, February 2026.
First-ever profit in FY26 (₹27.97 crore over three quarters); 4 million+ savings accounts opened within a year of full banking operations.
Raised $250 million+ from investors including Tiger Global, Insight Partners, and Advent International.
TAKEAWAY (pull-quote style, larger text)
A regulatory shock in 2022 nearly ended slice's original business — instead, it forced a pivot into a full, regulated banking licence, turning a near-death crisis into a more durable model.
